Date: 5 Sept. 2000

Home Shrine

Most of my work is done outside at the Tree Ring. Here is a glimpse of the pagan aspect of this home:

At the entrance to this house is the place considered especially sacred to Epona, a special protectress to this household. At the highest point above the inside of the front door to the porch is an inscription in ogham in Old Irish which may be translated, "Epona, watch over this household." She is called upon on leaving home and thanked on returning.

Below this and above the inside of that front door is the first list of names of ancestors. The names of my great-great grandparents are written above the inside of the outer door, the names of my great grand- parents are written above the outside of the inner door, the names of my grandparents are written above the inside of the inner door and the names of my parents are written above the outside of the entrance to the main part of the house from the vestibule. The names of my mother's Lavergne ancestors that are known back to the mid 1600s are placed below the inscription to Epona above the inside of the outer door.

On the eastern side above the opening between the living and dining rooms is a simple inscription in ogham saying, "Oghma". This is considered the central point in the house, and before this are performed those rituals here not conducted as a family at the dining room table.

On the west (dining room side) of that central point on which Oghma is written on the west is a large picture of a tree, stars and candles, drawn by Sheila, I believe when she was six. The tops of both book cases, i.e. on either side of this, contain genealogical files and family albums. Above the right (southern) bookcase is the blue egg made of wood which comes from an OPN ritual.

On the west wall of the kitchen, above the bookcase near the back door is a native good luck charm given to me years ago. Above this is my handwritten copy of a poem I wrote for an OPN ritual:

Manannan, Lyr's son, welcome be.
Come in peace from the Western Seas.
You and yours we greet gratefully.
Come favouring mortals and trees.
