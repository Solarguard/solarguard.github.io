Pan

One of the most prominent families through several generations of the Han Dynasty was the Ban (Pan) family. Michael McKenny has been very interested in this family for years. This subdirectory on this outstanding family contains a Ban (Pan) genealogical chart, a short review of the History of the Former Han Dynasty, written by Ban Gu (Pan Ku), his father and sister, a lengthier review in two files of the material in volume three of the Dubs' edition of that history, a review of Nancy Lee Swann's translation and comments on Pan Chao. This sub directory was created on December 30, 2003 C.E. and last updated on November 4, 2006 C.E. Note that these files were originally created when I was more familiar the with Wade Giles system of transliteration and refer to English material using that system.

1. Pan Genealogy A Pan genealogical chart with notes.

2. PAN CHAO: FOREMOST WOMAN SCHOLAR IN CHINA review of Nancy Lee Swann's fascinating study of this prominent Eastern Han scholar.

3. HISTORY OF THE FORMER HAN DYNASTY Brief review of Pan Ku's exemplary history, or that portion of it in the Dubs' edition.

4. Pan Ku on Wang Mang lengthier review of the material on Wang Mang in volume three of the Dubs' edition, effectively Pan Ku's chapters 11, 12 and 99 text, translation and introduction.

5. Pan Ku on Wang Mang's Economic Policy review of Dubs' material in v. 3 on these economic measures and on the kang-mao amulets.
