SAN KUO

SAN KUO, THREE KINGDOMS, is one of the treasures of global literature. It has been translated more than once into English. This site now provides a summary of each of the one hundred and twenty chapters of the earliest, and most literary, translation, the 1925 one by C. H. Brewitt-Taylor, and a summary of some of the supplementary material from Moss Roberts' 1991 translation. It will, in the course of time, attempt to provide extensive commentary and links to other SAN KUO sites. At present it also includes Michael McKenny's first review of SAN KUO written a few years ago and "Preliminary Observations." This subsection was created and the four files of chapter summaries placed here on November 19, 2003. It was last updated on December 3, 2007.

1. ROMANCE OF THE THREE KINGDOMS 1-30 Michael McKenny's Chapter summaries of the first thirty chapters of the earliest and most literary translation.

2. ROMANCE OF THE THREE KINGDOMS 31-60 Michael McKenny's Chapter summaries of the second thirty chapters of the earliest and most literary translation.

3. ROMANCE OF THE THREE KINGDOMS 61-90 Michael McKenny's Chapter summaries of the third thirty chapters of the earliest and most literary translation.

4. ROMANCE OF THE THREE KINGDOMS 91-120 Michael McKenny's Chapter summaries of the last thirty chapters of the earliest and most literary translation.

5. THREE KINGDOMS Michael McKenny's summary of some supplementary material from the 1991 translation by Moss Roberts.

6. Initial Review of the classic novel. This review was written by Michael McKenny at the end of 2001 C.E.

7. Preliminary Observations on the Emergence of Humanity as Exampled in the SAN KUO.
