History of the Former Han Dynasty

Pan Ku, transl. Homer H. Dubs, Waverly Press, Baltimore, v. 1 (1938), v. 2 (1944), v. 3 (1955)

It is hoped a fuller consideration of this major historical source will be posted to this site in future.

Pan Ku (d. 92 CE) was a member of an influential family. His history of the Former Han dynasty and of the reign of the usurper Wang Mang is quite fascinating. Actually, much of the extensive primary source material for Imperial Chinese history has yet to be translated, rendering this presentation of the annals section of Pan Ku's work very valuable.

There are many absorbing details for the reader interested in this subject. And, some general factors important throughout successive dynasties may be noted: the influence of families of the consorts, questions of succession, external relations, theory and practise in governance, imperial responses to portents, eunuchs, etc.

One of the details that seems to have touched the translator and may move many a reader is the incident where an imperial executioner is denied access to a prisoner and so that infant lived to become emperor. Dubs considers this emperor, Hsuan, "The best ruler in the whole former Han period." (v.2, p. 190) His reign did witness that visit by the Shan-yu of the Huns. "Such an event had never happened before, that the emperor of a powerful neighboring state should come to pay homage to a Chinese emperor." (v.2, p. 192)

Here are three very rich volumes. It is a goal of this site to provide more comment on them and on other early Chinese historical works.
